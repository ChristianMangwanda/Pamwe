# Pamwe voice guide

Derived 2026-07-12 from Christian's own writing in Notion (product ramblings,
reflective essays, goal pages, journals). Status: DRAFT, pending his sign-off
on the 12 calibration rewrites in copy-pass-plan.md. Once approved, every
user-facing string in the app gets written against this guide.

## Who is speaking

Pamwe speaks like a trusted friend who is walking the same road: plain,
warm, direct, and honest about the hard parts. Never a brand, never a pastor,
never an assistant. It talks TO the couple the way Christian talks to himself
on paper: short sentences, real words, no performance.

## The seven rules

1. **Say it plainly, in spoken cadence.** He writes like he talks: "Yeah we
   do need to make it faster." "Right now it is just not doing it." If a line
   would sound stiff said out loud across a kitchen table, rewrite it.

2. **Short declaratives, stacked.** He builds rhythm with periods, not
   clauses: "I do have a direction." / "Freezing is not killing." / "Do the
   great work first." Prefer two short sentences over one long one. A
   fragment is fine when it lands.

3. **Repetition with variation is his music.** "Try, try and bring this."
   "Grow and then grow again." "Closer to God. Closer to each other."
   Use sparingly, at the moments that matter (celebrations, milestones,
   taglines), not in body copy.

4. **Grace before instruction.** His self-talk marks failure without drama
   ("failed, got myself a new phone") and forgives fast ("that's still okay,
   no hard feelings honestly"). Error states and behind-states reassure
   FIRST, instruct second: "That's okay." "Don't worry, your words are
   saved." Never guilt, never urgency theater.

5. **Faith is personal and honest, never churchy.** His own faith writing
   admits distance ("I feel so far away from God") and weaves Scripture in
   naturally rather than performing it. Avoid devotional-brochure words
   (blessed, journey, abide, season) unless quoting Scripture. "Showing up"
   is his native register for faithfulness; "for Him" is as far as the copy
   reaches.

6. **Questions are welcome.** He thinks in rhetorical questions ("What's
   going on?", "What's the point?"). An occasional direct question in copy
   (onboarding, empty states) sounds like him. Don't stack more than one.

7. **End on the point.** His paragraphs close with a short line that carries
   the weight: "That is the whole point." "Do the great work first." "Only
   time will tell." Celebrations, milestones, and value slides should end on
   a 3-to-7-word closer, not trail off.

## Words he uses / words he doesn't

- YES: together, showing up, honestly, that's okay, no rush, the two of you,
  open, sealed, keep going, the whole point.
- NO: journey, unlock your potential, experience (as a noun for the app),
  seamless, oops/whoops/uh-oh, we're sorry (say what happened instead),
  exclamation marks doing fake excitement (one "!" max per screen, earned).
- Warmth is allowed to be tender ("my love" appears in his own writing) but
  app copy addresses the couple, so tenderness goes through "together" and
  "the two of you", not pet names.

## Mechanics (hard rules)

- No em dashes anywhere. Commas, colons, periods.
- Straight apostrophes everywhere (normalize the curly ones).
- Null placeholder glyph is `·`.
- Contractions on: you're, it's, we'll, don't. (His writing contracts
  naturally; the stiff spots in current copy are where it stopped.)
- Scripture text, M'Cheyne pull quotes, and reflection prompts are never
  touched.
- Push notifications: title states the fact, body gives the one next step.
  Both under ~10 words.

## Register map (which rule leads where)

| Surface | Leads with |
|---|---|
| Onboarding / value slides | Rule 3 + 7: rhythm, end on the point |
| Instructions / placeholders | Rule 1: plain spoken, one thought |
| Waiting / sealed states | Rule 2 + 7: short declaratives, close with why it's worth it |
| Errors / offline | Rule 4: grace first, then the step |
| Behind / catch-up | Rule 4: "that's okay" register, zero guilt |
| Celebrations / milestones | Rule 3 + 5: repetition, "showing up", end on the point |
| Push notifications | Rule 1 + 2: fact, then step |
| Ask Pamwe fixed lines | Rule 1 + 6: plain, can carry one question, never preachy |
| Plan taglines | Rule 3: parallel rhythm, 3-6 words |
